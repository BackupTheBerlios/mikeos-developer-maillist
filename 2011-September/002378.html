Hi Mike,

You do of course realize that all disk access routines
in the kernel would also have to be changed to FAT16.

OK, I will have a look at it.

Jumping forward a bit, the easiest way to access the disk
is to use INT 13h as you know, however when it applies to
HDDs (dl = 0x80h or 0x81h) , only the newer BIOSes
support INT13h extensions for LBA 48.
What this means is that to make the new MikeOS more
compatible with older hardware, we have to limit HDD
access to LBA 28, which in turn places an 8GB limit.
I really don't see this as a problem.

Paulo.
