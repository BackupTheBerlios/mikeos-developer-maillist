On Fri, 9 Sep 2011 23:37:51 +0100, M Saunders wrote:
> MikeOS is a learning tool for those interested in OS development.
I would assume that one of the goals of any learning tool is that it 
(and, if open source like MikeOS, its code) should not be needlessly 
complex. While I am partially guilty of adding a level of complexity by 
adding a new and improved shell, I am of the opinion that having a 
self-extracting-emulator-with-preloaded-images is just... out of the 
scope of the project itself. Maybe as a standalone project (like how 
WUBI downloads and installs a dual-boot on Windows with GRUB to load a 
hard drive image running your favourite flavour of Ubuntu), it might 
work, but the existence of such a thing would probably stay separate 
from the main MikeOS project itself.

Not to mention that it would add a good 10-15 MB to the ZIP, which is 
only 227 KB for 4.1.2.

Cheers.
