Wouldn't make much difference as MikeOS doesn't have a malloc
implementation nor a C-like stdio! :-)

Forget about on-screen lines, and think in terms of memory. Say, for
now, that files can be a maximum 512 lines long. Define 512 words
which describe where in RAM the lines reside (ie a table of line
pointers), and 512 corresponding words which describe line length.
That's 2K of overhead -- but at least you're not eating up RAM for
blank lines.

Then you can update these pointers as you insert new lines or remove
them. Your text data can be a solid stream of characters somewhere in
RAM. For instance, if line 15 of 30 is removed, 16, 17, 18+ move back
in RAM and in the table.

That's the quickest way to do it as a line editor (you'll also need a
little command parser for 'E 3' (edit), 'D 15' (delete) etc.
