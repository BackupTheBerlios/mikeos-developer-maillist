>  # New system call: os_filename_convert -- takes a normal filename such
>  as 'FOO.BIN' and converts it into the FAT12 equivalent of 'FOO
>  BIN' (first part padded out with spaces to 8 chars, no full-stop
>  separator)

That may have wrapped badly in the email :-) Just to clarify:

We're used to dealing with filenames such as 'FOO.BIN' or 'HELLO.YOU'.
However, FAT12 is more restrictive, in that filenames have to be 11
characters long exactly (8 for the first bit and 3 for the extension).
Any letters that you don't want to use are replaced with spaces in the
FAT12 directory entries. So you have:

'FOO.BIN' becomes 'FOO     BIN' (five spaces after FOO)

and

'HELLO.YOU' becomes 'HELLO   YOU' (three spaces after HELLO)

It's a bit fiddly to work with, hence why I've written the
os_filename_convert system call for 1.9.4.
