Yeah, assembly does take a certain way of thinking. It's pretty
strange at first but you soon get used to it. There are a LOT of
instructions that you don't need to use unless you're writing a
compiler -- the majority of MikeOS involves mov, cmp, jmp and math
instructions.

Get used to things you do in C all the time: loops, comparisons,
calling functions with parameters (eg passed in registers). Without
the standard library, C is just a thin and portable wrapper around
assembly, hence why you can still mess around with individual bytes in
RAM via pointers.
