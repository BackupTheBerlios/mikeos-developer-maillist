The BIOS is all well and good from a simplicity perspective, but from what
I've read (and from what we seem to be finding out) it can be a real
limitting factor for 32-bit (as you still need to send 16-bit instructions
to the BIOS - it is an ancient piece of technology after all!).

I think the biggest breakthrough for MikeOS will be when it finally breaks
away from the BIOS, but that of course would mean writing proper drivers in
ASM. To be fair, you could probably take samples from bkerndev and James M's
kernel - they may be written in C but it is not beyond anyone to re-write
something similar in your preferred lingo ;)

Food for thought...
