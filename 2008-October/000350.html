Judging by my translated version of the site (thank you google
translator...!) there are a few things people don't seem to get:
a) Why it's in 16-bit
b) Why it's in assembler

The answers to both are clear. a) is easier when you're trying to learn b)
which gives you more control over your kernel.

I personally prefer to use C, but learning assembler from MikeOS is helping
a lot with porting stuff to ApolloOS and gives you an insight into how
kernels really work at a fundamental level.

I think once it's graduated to 32-bit protected mode (thus needing drivers)
it would cease to be the learning tool it's intended to be - but it would be
neat to be running an office suite and to surf the net on MikeOS one day in
the future! :D
