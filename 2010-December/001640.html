I have no idea what you are aiming at here, but I`ll hazard a guess.

MikeOS has NO access to graphics modes as it uses Video Mode 3, CGA, (under
emulation these days), text; emulated as 720x400x4bits normally.

Under M/C code you do have access, depending on the graphics card, (also under
emuation), from Video Modes 0 to 19 decimal, (less 11 and 12). Note that very few
machines can emulate Tandy Modes 8, 9 and 10.

(I doubt that current graphics cards can access the really strange IBM XT CGA
graphics games modes at all.)

The standard modes are all available from BIOS INT 10h.

mov ax,<word_length_video_mode_in_hex>
int 10h

To return back to MikeOS I am now using jmp 0h to restart the kernel.

I might be barking up the wrong tree but I hope that helps.
