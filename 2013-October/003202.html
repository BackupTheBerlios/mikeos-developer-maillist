Jagged O'Neill wrote:

> First, I don't use NASM but GAS; readers tend to miss the '%' prefix
> on registers; why and how to in the header comment.

I also use GAS.
I prefer to use intel syntax (.intel_syntax noprefix).
That way NASM and FASM users can easily understand the (much cleaner
syntax) code. Also, capitalize the filename extention (S) and pass
it to gcc to use the 'C' preprocessor before it is passed to GAS.
This means 'C' comments (/* */, //), #define and "\r\n" etc.
