I agree with your point that simplicity is MikeOS's biggest asset.

It's true that you need to be in pmode to properly implement memory  
protection. What we could do perhaps is to show people how to do what  
Ian's suggested as an optional measure people can take in the handbook  
but keep the actual code clean.

That leads to my idea - though we've stripped a lot out of MikeOS, why  
not have a section in the handbook about how to put them back in (like  
a section saying 'How can I extend MikeOS?'). It would allow new  
developers to follow tutorials, actively learn how to develop MikeOS  
and then be able to build their own extensions and solutions using the  
experience they've gained.

It's food for thought anyway...
