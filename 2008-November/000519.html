Nope ain't the same Paul Sommers.

I quite like the idea of introducing "how to" guides for developers
wishing to put back some of the features taken out of MikeOS.
Dos compatibility comes to mind.
Surely I can't be the only one with old favourites which I'd like to use
under MikeOS.

I understand that not all the Dos INTs have been ported but what I've
started to do is the following:

1) Open the Dos app under a debugger/disassembler and make a note of the
INTs used.
2) Add them into os_dos.asm file.

Just keep in mind that quite a few Dos apps are actually compressed with exe
packers/compressors
such as UPX , PKLITE and of course Microsoft used their own when pgms are
compiled with QB4.5
Make sure you decompress them before disassembly.
To see which compressor has been used, open the executable in a hex editor
and look in the first 500
bytes or so.
There will be ascii text with the name of the compressor.

The  decompresors are available at:

http://upx.sourceforge.net/   and Pklite
ftp://ftp.simtel.net/pub/simtelnet/msdos/execomp/pklts201.zip

For more links for other decompresses:

http://www.bookcase.com/library/software/msdos.archive.compress.html

For the disassembler I use a nice simple one called D86 (actually part of
A86) available here:

http://eji.com/a86.zip

One could always use "debug" which comes with every single Microsoft OS
since Dos and even under XP
Not too sure about Vista.

Don't have any info for Linux as I don't use it.

All the best,
Paul.

P.S.

What I would really like to see is FAT16 support for MikeOS, although I
understand the extra complexity
it would add.
