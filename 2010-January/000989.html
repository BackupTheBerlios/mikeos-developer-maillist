Hi Bazza,

What I meant by direct port access is that it does not have to be done via the kernel
as is the case with windows.
In windows, the LPT is "owned" by the kernel which in turn hands exclusive control
to the print spooler service.
However this is easy to turn off and with the aid of a dll running in Ring 0,  "direct"
port access is possible.

As far as IrDA is concerned, it's actually easier then you might imagine.
Remember that the fancy protocols used by IrDA are only required when the port
is being used at very fast speeds of 4MB/s
At lower speeds (i.e 9600) raw access is perfectly acceptable and the physical layer
has many similaraties to a standard serial port hence loads of memory and coding is not
required to simulate a good old fashioned com port.

I have played around with using different segments by loading them into the ES and DS regs
with no problems what so ever.
Just watch what you do with CS.

Be more optimistic Bazza....remember what you said about USB keyboards ?
