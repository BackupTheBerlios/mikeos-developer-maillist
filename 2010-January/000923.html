Hi Bazza,

I guess not all pc's/netbooks/laptops have IrDA, but like you said it's another doorway.
Have you tried a USB keyboard ?
Since a keyboard is part of the HID class, I suspect that BIOS has support for it and should work
straight "out the box".
If this is so, then you could always "hack" the keyboard and connect your inputs to certain keys
via adequate isolation and presto, crude input port.
If you needed output then you could always write to the keyboard controller to switch on and off the CAPS LOCK and NUM LOCK leds.
This could emulate the serial clock and chip select for your ADC chips.

Paulo.
