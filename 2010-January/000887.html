Hi all,

Mike, have you ever considered using unreal mode for MikeOS?
Since most computers these days (and even of several years ago) probably
have at least 128MB of RAM or more, it seems a shame to be limited to 640KB and
the horrible sector:offset arrangement with all those overlapping segments.

Now using protected mode is a real pain since BIOS functions are no longer accessible
and writing a whole lot of drivers ain't too much fun, not to mention all that Ring 0, Ring 1 nonsense.

Enter Unreal mode....basically switch to protected mode, set just one GDT, enable the A20 line
then switch back to real mode.
What this does is allow 32 bit addressing and hence access up to 4GB of RAM (assuming that much is actually installed on a pc)
but since we switch back to real mode, the BIOS functions are available again.
This is a well known and unofficially documented bug/feature of the x86 family since the 386.

The stack segment and code segment are still limited to 64KB but the data segment is now one large linear chunk of up to 4GB.
What do you reckon Mike? up to it for MikeOS 4.5?

Paulo.
