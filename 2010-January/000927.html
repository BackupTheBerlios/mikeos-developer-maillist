Bazza,

I think you misunderstood was I was getting at.
The BIOS will by default support a USB keyboard connect to a USB port and translate
it's data as if it were a normal PS2 keyboard.
You don't have to enable it.(it was also confirmed by Sasano)

What this means is that all MikeOS calls to the keyboard buffer will work as usual.
So all that is left to do is physically connect your circuitry to a key so that when your circuit gives an output
it will be translated into a keypress which your software can then do what it needs with it.

Paulo.
