Hi folks,

> Is it due to cost? (I doubt it.)

Ultimately, yes. Of course, these ports cost a pittance compared to
the overall price of an average PC/laptop. But it's all about margins.
For years the price of PCs has come down. There's a global economic
crisis. With these factors, most companies in the PC-selling market
are affected.

Imagine that you're Product Manager X of a PC shipping firm. You've
been tasked by CEO Y to reduce the manufacturing cost of your boxes by
Z percent. What's the first thing you do? Look for all the stuff the
vast majority don't use and shave it off -- saving pennies perhaps,
but it all adds up and pleases the company bigwigs.

You may remember the old story: "AMERICAN AIRLINES saved $40,000 in
1987 by eliminating one olive from each salad served in first class."

An olive seems totally trivial price-wise -- like a parallel port --
but altogether, over huge numbers of units with transportation and
integration etc. involved it all adds up over a year! So for olive
fans (and us low-level hackers) it sucks, but it's understandable why
it happens.
