Paulo,

Just dd-ed your latest .ima to my USB key and tried to boot it on my real PC:

"Error Executing FAT16 bootsector
Press any key to reboot"

That's generated by code in your bootsector, right? So the boot sector
itself has loaded OK -- problem must be after that.

Meanwhile I just started from scratch and got somewhere. I generated a
16MB image, ran "mkdosfs" on it and took the values from the BPB. I
then took the FreeDOS bootloader we linked to and put the BPB bytes
inside it. It loaded and jumped to the kernel straight away --
although the kernel fell over on the get_random_seed bit.

Still, I added code to print "LOL" to the MikeOS kernel at startup,
and this was displayed, so I've got a bootloader that loads and jumps
to the kernel OK, and works on QEMU and real hardware.
