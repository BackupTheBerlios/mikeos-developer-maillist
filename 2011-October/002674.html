I know, but I cannot see RM16 disappearing
overnight due to compatibility issues.
it will be a case of either boot from UEFI
or from BIOS.
Reading about UEFI, it seems that it even uses
"traditional" BIOS to do the POST checks and generally
boot up the motherboard.

If you want to experiment with the advantages
of RM16 but have access to 4GB memory
then why not experiment with UNREAL mode.
It basically involves switching to PM then making
the GS register 0 then switching back to RM.

Some people say that some BIOS functions may
"destroy" the GS register but in practice I have
never found that to be the case.
