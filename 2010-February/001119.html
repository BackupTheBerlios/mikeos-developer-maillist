> http://www.xs4all.nl/~smit/asm01001.htm

I haven't read it all so I don't want to slate it completely, but I'm
not sure how good that tutorial is for beginners.

"First of all, we'll talk about the registers and then about the
instructions to manipulate (change) them. The 8086 has 14 16-bit
registers..."

At no point does it explain what registers actually are! Are they
variables? Where are they stored? What do they do? I think it's
important to have a mental picture of the overall workings of the CPU
beforehand: it has a math unit, it has onboard registers for
short-term data storage, and it interfaces with RAM for longer-term
data storage etc.

It's great that the tutorial gets right to the meat of the matter, but
from a cursory glance it makes a lot of assumptions about prior
knowledge and could confuse beginners!

Just my two pennies :-)
