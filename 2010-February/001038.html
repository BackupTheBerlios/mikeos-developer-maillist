Mike,

Keep in mind that not all USB flash keys are created equal.
On some it's a piece of cake to make bootable, whilst  on others it's next to impossible.
So much for standards.

HP has made a windows app to make their flash keys bootable.
It's reported to work with other models too.
I've not tried this app extensively so I cannot say if it will fit the bill.
It's available here:

http://h20000.www2.hp.com/bizsupport/TechSupport/SoftwareDescription.jsp?swItem=MTX-UNITY-I23839

How would one be able to save the a file to the flash disk?
Would you include support for FAT16 as I suspect that most flash drives emulate some sort of hard-drive and windows
refuses to format any drive bigger than 2GB to anything other than FAT32.
Smaller drives can be formated to FAT16.

Paulo.
