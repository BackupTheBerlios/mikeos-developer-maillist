Like Tslil, I'm just throwing some ideas around here...

Recently on Slashdot (or similar) I saw a story bemoaning the
complexity of computers thesedays and how hard they are for kids to
learn. Sure, there are fairly easy-to-grasp languages like Python, but
you can still get bogged down in the multiple levels of abstraction in
a modern OS, and the billions of libraries for these languages.

One poster remarked that he was going to dig out his old C64 for his
kid to start programming on, like he did. Some others expressed
similar feelings. Perhaps there's a demand for something here?
