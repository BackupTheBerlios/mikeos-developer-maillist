I agree Paulo. Although with a CD version you can't save your work, so
I think flash keys are the way to go. Everyone has one (or a few), and
the low-end ones are cheap as chips now. We could even buy 20 or so
and sell them on the site.

Ideally we'd get the help of a Windows programmer who'd make a single
.exe that you double-click and it turns your inserted USB key into a
bootable MikeOS drive. Couldn't be simpler!

(On Linux we could have a .sh which contains the zipped up data at the
end and writes it out in response to a couple of questions.)

So yes, unlike MikeOS where you're expected to be interested in the
source, with NEWBASICOS you'd just get the double-click thing for your
platform.
