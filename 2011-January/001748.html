On Wed, Jan 12, 2011 at 9:46 AM, Raghav Sood <raghavsood at gmail.com> wrote:
>
> Thanks I will do as you suggest. I hope I can get this going. I have another
> question though. If I get a GUI working through the BIOS will it still work
> on a computer that initializes using UEFI? (That's the expected replacement
> to the BIOS).

Probably not, sadly. UEFI is a totally different beast, and doesn't
appear to provide anything like the same easy access to (certain)
hardware like the BIOS. Perhaps some UEFI implementations will emulate
BIOS interrupt support, but given that 99.99% of PCs sold today end up
running Win/Lin/BSD/Hackintosh, there's not much point.

Hopefully the BIOS will still be around for a few more years, but
after that it gets tricky. We could move MikeOS over to use UEFI but
it'd involve rewriting large parts of the OS, adding complexity
through drivers and changing what makes it special IMHO.
