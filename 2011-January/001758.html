On Wed, Jan 12, 2011 at 11:06 AM, Raghav Sood <raghavsood at gmail.com> wrote:
>
> few people take you seriously. Would there be a way to store the characters
> as binary in auth.txt, read them into the RAM and then check it? Binary
> would offer some very very basic protection.

Hrm, not clear what you mean here. How would you convert ASCII text
into binary? Do you mean the user would enter password "foo" and it
would store (literally) "1001010110100101010010" in the AUTH.TXT file?

That's still ASCII characters by the way, and still editable. Given
that there are no permissions in MikeOS (and there's no reason to), I
would implement some sort of message digest routine. Take a password,
generate a hash from it, and store it in AUTH.TXT. When user is asked
for password, get hash and compare with that in AUTH.TXT.

That way, users can't see cleartext passwords in AUTH.TXT. This is a
complex subject though, and takes some reading:

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryptographic_hash_function

Also it's way beyond the scope of this OS-devel list, so please post
any further questions about hashing etc. to a more relevant
forum/list!
