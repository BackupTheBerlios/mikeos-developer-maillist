> Are you saying that GRUB or similar has it's own drivers to read the keyboard

> and does not rely on BIOS at all?

GRUB at the moment uses BIOS calls. However, it appears that EFI
bootloaders are expected to be written as EFI applications -- ie
programs loaded inside EFI itself. So this further complicates
matters. While EFI applications can seemingly request simple stuff
such as having messages printed on screen, there's no clarity whether
external OSes can use these calls. And there certainly appears to be
no direct mapping for disk calls, like floppy drive sector access
essential to MikeOS.

Then there's this: "UEFI runs in uniprocessor flat 64-bit long mode".
So, it still looks like MikeOS would have to be mostly rewritten and
redesigned, with many of its own drivers, to work on UEFI machines.
Here's hoping the BIOS will stay around for a while...
