On 05.12.2011 13:29, Joshua Beck wrote:
> Is there a formula I can use to find out the number to send to
> os_speaker_tone to get an exact sound frequency?
Presuming you just send a frequency in Hz through, say, AX to 
os_speaker_tone, you can use the following table: 
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_pitch_notation#Table_of_note_frequencies

You could also use scientific pitch, where to shift up or down an 
octave you just do a bitwise shift left or right respectively, but A 
above middle C is 431 Hz in scientific pitch as opposed to 440 Hz in 
concert (standard) pitch.

A431 and A440 actually do sound quite different: 
http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bb/Tone_430-54Hz.ogg vs 
http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/50/Sine_wave_440.ogg 
(both can be rather loud/startling if you're not prepared for them - 
they're both sine waves) so you're best to use the usual A440 concert 
pitch if you're going to do renditions of songs and such.


Best way to do all of this in MikeOS would be a lookup table since we 
can't properly do logarithms (if at all) without floating point. You 
could implement a table with 128 notes in 256 bytes, and that would 
encompass an entire 88-key piano keyboard and then some.
