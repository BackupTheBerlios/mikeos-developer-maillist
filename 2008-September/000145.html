On Fri, Sep 26, 2008 at 3:43 PM, Troy Martin <blasterman95 at gmail.com> wrote:
>
> I've tried my share of writing cross compilers (don't ask, please don't) and
> now I think it would be easier to write a standard compiler that runs on
> MikeOS.

It wouldn't be any easier making a native MikeOS compiler. You still
have to spit out machine code (and have a VERY good knowledge of the
x86 CPU), but at least on a Unix/Win host you have more libraries to
help you in the implementation of the compiler.

> Now I just need to know how to parse files.

And lexical analysis, and preprocessing, and multi-pass compiling, and
LOTS of other complicated stuff... I'm not saying it's not worthwile,
but as with TCP/IP support and many other things you've mentioned, I
think you've radically underestimated how complex this is, and how
much experience (especially with the x86 architecture) you need!

A QBASIC-like _interpreter_, however, is much more feasible :-)
