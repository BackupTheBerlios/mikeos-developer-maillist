On Thu, Feb 3, 2011 at 9:45 AM, Mike Gonta <mikegonta at gmail.com> wrote:
>
> aeBIOS to the rescue!

You're absolutely right, and I think your project is brilliant. I'm
just wondering what benefits we'd get from having MikeOS go 32-bit
protected mode as the default.

We wouldn't have to worry about the 64K segment limit, but then
nobody's writing big memory-hogging apps on MikeOS. I guess my fear
is: it'd introduce a magical black-box between the OS and the
hardware.

Right now, when you learn how MikeOS works and make modifications, you
have your code running directly on the bare metal. You can understand
exactly what each part of the OS does -- there's just your code and
the PC. Adding a layer between that complicates things, and means
there's something else you have to learn, to get a grand picture of
everything. It's not quite as "pure" as before.

But that's just my view. I'd be interested to hear any pro-protected
mode arguments!
