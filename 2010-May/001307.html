Hi Bazza,

Are you thinking what I'm thinking as regards a PS2 mouse?
It would make a wonderful "input" device to complement your keyboard I/O project.

They have 2 buttons, some 3, a scroll wheel and X,Y movement.
So why not get a USB one (they are cheap enough) throw the casing and all the cogs and little wheels out,
put the pcb in a new plastic project box add a bit of circuitry and 
Bob's your uncle!

Since you are using it as a general purpose "input" device and not an actual mouse,
you wouldn't even have to make the code TSR.
Just read the port in a loop as per this url:

http://wiki.osdev.org/Mouse_Input

The BIOS would take care of the emulation as it does with the USB keyboard.
There you go, yet another option for I/O on modern pc's with no ports on them except USB.

Paulo.
