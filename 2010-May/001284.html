Hi all,

Thanks for all your ideas and suggestions.
The usb flash drive is now booting with MikeOS on it.

This is what I did:

1) Set all sectors to zero using winhex.
I don't know how important this step is but I did it anyway.
I believe dd also has a setting to do this.

2) As per MikeGonta's suggestion I then renamed dd.exe to dd-removeable.exe

3) Then typed in dd-removable.exe --list to get all the drives info.

4) Again as per MikeGonta's suggestion I used the following parameters with dd-removable.exe:

dd-removable if=c:\mikeos\mikeos41.ima of=\\?\Harddrive1\Partition0

(obviously change the of=\\?\....... to suit your flash drive).

5) Rebooted the pc and all was fine.

Please note that depending on your BIOS, you might have to play around with the settings of the first boot device.
The settings are:

USB-HDD, USB-FDD and USB-ZIP.

Mine wanted USB-FDD

There you go.
By the way the USB flash drive I used is the INTEGRAL 2GB (usb 2.0)

Paulo.
