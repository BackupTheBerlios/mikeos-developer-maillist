Hi Paulo...

> Why not use the CAPSLOCK, NUMLOCK and SCROLL LOCK LED signals
> as CLOCK,DATA, and STROBE signals feeding one or more shift registers.
> In this way you can have as many TTL/CMOS outputs as you like.

Simplicity. I always build as though I am an 8 to 10 year old who would not
be very good a building on a kitchen table AND have a very short attention
span...

> For inputs, you could use transistors connected to the F1 to F12 keys as these
> are hardly ever used in MikeOS.

What addresses do they sit on for DMA?

Address 0040:0017 is directly accessible for:-

Left Caps Shift
Right Caps Shift
Alt
Ctrl

For real time read only, (in my case both Caps Shifts)...

Caps Lock
Scroll Lock, (sometimes not usable!)
Num Lock
Insert, (not usable!)

For toggled I/O but I am using it for O only, (on Caps Lock and Num Lock)...

These are more than enough for I/O so that`s where I stopped.

Plus I wanted the KB intact for development use so the wiring for your method
would make the KB unusable.

Lastly all the components were raided from old equipment, (the junk box :)
and I don`t want to use any ICs at all, just bipolar transistors and diodes...

BTW have you finished the IrDA I/O yet?
