> Which BIOSes use hard drive emulation, and which floppy drive? Can't
> they do both?

Some BIOS cannot boot from USB at all (these are older machines
with USB). Historically booting with floppy drive emulation only
came first. Some later machines apparently can boot from either
depending on the setup. However just because the BIOS has a
boot from HDD option this means removable hard drive and not
flash drive. Newer machines only boot flash drives with hard
drive emulation.

The boot process is simple, the BIOS loads the boot sector to
absolute address 7C00h and runs. Apparently there are reports
that the BIOS makes a determination based on the file structure
and/or content. I have seen no proof of this.
You do not need an MBR to boot a hard drive and a FAT12
formated hard drive boots fine.
