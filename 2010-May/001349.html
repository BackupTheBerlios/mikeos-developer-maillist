Bazza,

>There is no way of knowing even with your code how many REAL clock
>pulses have passed unless you know ALL the the instruction set clock timings
>per instruction.

Theoretically yes,  but keep in mind that the granularity of the INT 1Ah sub-function 0 is 55mS.
Even on a 386 33MHz (30nS), the opcode execution time is insignificant compared to 55mS.
So even if the loop takes 200 clock cycles it's only 6 microseconds.

I agree that if you were using it to accurately measure frequency then 6 microseconds is a lot,
but for counting in chunks of 110ms, I don't see it as a problem.
