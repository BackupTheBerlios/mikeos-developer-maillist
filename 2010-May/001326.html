Hi all,

I suspect that the answer to the missing INT 15h on some machines lies in the excerpt from 
http://www.drdobbs.com/embedded-systems/199500688;jsessionid=FBSQKVT23JSKFQE1GHPSKHWATMY32JVN?pgno=3

"For example, the venerable Int15h interface would pass the service type in AX. 
Since no central repository or specification managed the evolution of Int15h services, 
several vendors defined similar service numbers, thus making interoperability 
with operating systems and pre-OS applications difficult."

What I read into the above is don't rely on INT 15h on new machines and especially those whose manufactures belong
to the UEFI organization and make a BIOS with (U)EFI support of some kind.(such as the D410PT from Intel)

To see which do belong to it, check out http://www.uefi.org/join/list

Paulo.
