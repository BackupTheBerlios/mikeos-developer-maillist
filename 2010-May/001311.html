Bazza,

> Also thought about MikeOS but the problem is it only has input so I decided against it.

This is not always a problem.
Say for example you wanted to demonstrate how to monitor if say 3 lights at a remote location (the basement)
were on or off.

Get hold of three current transformers, place in series with each of the "loads", rectify their output, clamp to max 5v
then feed into each of the optos shown on my previously posted schematic.

With some simple code you could get MikeOS to monitor the status of the three lights or any other loads.
You could even expand on the idea and add a 8 to 3 encoder chip and you could monitor 8 things just by using a converted USB mouse.

So depending on application, the fact that it's input only is not a disadvantage.
Plus it would add extra inputs to your keyboard project as by reading addresses 60h and 64h one could easily check if "data" came from the keyboard or mouse.

When I get some spare time I'm off to my local PC shop to get hold of a couple of cheap USB rodents which will become general purpose digital inputs for my MikeOS machine.
