Hi folks,

A couple of things I've been thinking about here:

1) MikeOS is a learning platform, and not written specifically to
target very old machines. It's good that it does work on some
old-school kit, but that's a by-product of it being a simple x86
assembly language OS rather than anything intentional.

2) Currently, we have an implementation of os_pause that is broken on
some modern BIOSes. People are going out and buying PCs with these
BIOSes -- they are becoming much more common. Some people who have
these PCs will get interested in OS development, and our learning OS
project should definitely work right for them.

If this problem hinged around, say, Pentium II processors, it would be
tricky. But really, if we can stick a "needs a 386-25+" label on
MikeOS and it's guaranteed to work properly, that's absolutely fine.
Anything below that is supremely old, and I'm not knocking that, as I
love old kit (I did some Spectrum Z80 coding recently!).

However, as mentioned, this is a learning tool rather than a project
to bring new life out of extremely old hardware, so I think a 386-25
cut-off point is already low enough that it won't effect 99.999% of
people who'd ever be interested in MikeOS.

Cheers,
Mike
