Hi,

Could this perhaps be the "in"-famous bug with some BIOS'
wrongly translating the absolute address 7C00h?
I have read reports where this is the case.

See here http://forum.osdev.org/viewtopic.php?f=1&p=166859

About halfway down the page:

" Instead of talking about addressing types (which I'm sure the OP can find in the Intel manuals) 
I'll get back to the original problem. 
Yes, I agree that there are BIOSes out there that pass execution control to 07C0:0000h instead of 0000:7C00h 
(which is the address defined by the BDA standard). 
BIOSes that do this are borked but you shouldn't have much problems with that anyway 
since all your jumps will probably be relative."

I also had a look at the FAT12 bootloader of DexOS which has the following:

;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;
;; Copy ourself to top of memory ;;
;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;;

    mov    cx, 256
    mov    si, 7C00h
    xor    di, di
    mov    ds, di
    rep    movsw

Where as the MikeoS bootloader has:

mov ax, 07C0h

I'm totally confused now.
Are they pointing to the same address?

Paulo.
