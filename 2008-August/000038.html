Lo Bob,

Regarding SourceForge, you can use SCP (SSH file copy) to upload your
script to SourceForge. For instance, grab PSCP from
http://www.chiark.greenend.org.uk/~sgtatham/putty/download.html and
the command will be something like this:

pscp myfile.php
sfusername at shell.sourceforge.net:/home/groups/a/ap/apolloopsys/htdocs

(That's all one line.) Replace sfusername with, of course, your
SourceForge username. You'll be prompted for your password. This will
then copy myfile.php into your HTML docs directory.

If you redirect with a script, search engines won't necessarily pick
it up. That's why, for my SF account (they're being slow to remove
it), I've just got a simple HTML page with a link:

http://mikeos.sourceforge.net

If you get stuck with SCP, you can always make me (mksaunders) an
admin on your SF project, and then I can upload your changes!
